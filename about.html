The Founder's Story

Why Ler Wee Meng Started Securevision

Most security companies are started by salespeople. Securevision was started by an engineer with a law degree — and that difference shapes everything we do.

After more than a decade working across Singapore's security systems industry, our founder Ler Wee Meng saw the same problem repeating: security systems that detected problems but couldn't tell you what was wrong. An alarm fires. Someone gets a call. But nobody knows if it's a break-in, a sensor fault, or a cat crossing the yard.

He called it the "Blind Alarm Problem" — and in 2006, he founded Securevision specifically to solve it. The approach was different from the start: assess the site like an engineer, design the system like an architect, and stand behind it like a professional with something to lose.

His engineering background means every system we design is specified for how it actually needs to work — not what's easiest to install. His legal background means we understand the regulatory framework, the liability implications, and the compliance requirements our clients operate under.

  • Every system proposal is signed off by a qualified engineer — not a sales rep
  • We hold a Police Licence under the Private Security Industry Act — legally required, not optional
  • Our contracts are clear on what we deliver, when, and what happens if we don't
  • We answer the phone after handover — because our reputation depends on it

Systems, Not Products

A camera that doesn't talk to your access control isn't a security system. We design systems where every component serves a defined role.
