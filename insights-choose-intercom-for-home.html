How to Choose an Intercom for Your Home

An intercom is the first point of contact between your home and the outside world. Getting the specification right means understanding six decisions before you look at a single brand or model.

Ler Wee Meng Founder & CEO · 37+ Years Experience
  • Home
  • Insights
  • Security Planning
  • How to Choose an Intercom for Your Home

1. Start with how you actually use the entrance

Before you look at any product, answer three questions about how your property is used.

  • How many entry points do visitors use? A landed home with multiple gates has different requirements than a condo unit.
  • Who is typically home? If adults are present, wired handsets work. If empty, you need mobile integration.
  • Are you renovating or retrofitting? Renovation allows new cables; retrofits must work with existing wiring.

2. Decision 1: How many door stations do you need?

A door station is the unit mounted outside. It contains the call button, microphone, speaker, and camera. It also controls the gate lock relay.

Most Singapore landed homes need one door station. Larger properties with separate car and pedestrian gates need two. Not all systems support multiple stations — Kocom single-stations differ from Aiphone or Akuvox multi-point systems. Check support early.

3. Decision 2: How many indoor substations?

Indoor units (handsets or screens) should be placed where you spend most of your time — living room, kitchen, and near staircases. One per floor is a sensible minimum for multi-storey landed homes.

Most wired systems support 2-3 units. Aiphone can handle up to 8. Akuvox IP systems have no practical limit because each unit is a network device. Factor this into your selection to avoid system capacity limits.

4. Decision 3: Audio only or video?

Video intercoms add a camera, allowing you to vet visitors before opening. It also serves as a security recording point, capturing faces at gate level rather than heads from a roofline CCTV.

Audio is cheaper and simpler but offers less security vetting. For most Singapore landed homes where the gate is set back from the building, video is a critical security upgrade.

KEY POINT

A video intercom door station positioned at gate height captures faces. A CCTV camera positioned at roofline captures the tops of heads. For identifying visitors, the intercom camera is often more useful.

5. Decision 4: Do you need mobile phone access?

Mobile access routes calls to your smartphone. You can see, speak, and open the gate from anywhere. This is invaluable for working households managing deliveries or family members monitoring elderly occupants remotely.

IP-based systems like Akuvox connect via ethernet or SIM card, using apps to handle communication regardless of your location.

6. Decision 5: What credential controls the gate?

Basic systems open when you press a button. Advanced units like the Akuvox R29 include card readers, PIN pads, or face recognition. You can issue temporary codes to visitors that expire automatically, eliminating the need to be present for every arrival.

7. Decision 6: What cable do you have, and does it matter?

For renovations, run Cat6. For retrofits, identify existing 2-core or 4-core cable. Some modern systems (Akuvox 2-wire) can use legacy cables, while most IP systems require Cat6. Verify compatibility before purchase to avoid expensive re-cabling.

The most common mistake is choosing based on appearance without verifying system logic. Work through these six decisions systematically, then find the product that fits your infrastructure and needs.
