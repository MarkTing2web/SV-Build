## ⚠️ RULES SPECIFIC TO THIS PAGE TYPE

1. **No inline `<style>` blocks.** Brand pages use `sv-shared.css` only — do NOT load `sv-guides.css`.
2. **Tone is partnership, not promotion.** We explain why Securevision specifies this brand and where it fits — not advertising copy for the manufacturer.
3. **Never use superlatives without a verifiable proof point.** "World's largest manufacturer" (Hikvision — verifiable) is permitted. "Best alarm system" is not.
4. **Scope declaration is mandatory.** Every page must state exactly which products Securevision integrates. If the brand makes more than we use, say so professionally.
5. **Brand tier determines page tone.** See the three-tier table below — Tier 2 pages are conversion pages, not product pages.
6. **Section 3 shows 3 representative models only** — within scope, from the BOM provided. Not a catalogue.
7. **Section 4 links to Solution pages only** — not back to brand or system pages.

## 3. SECTION 1 — ABOUT THE BRAND

Three distinct parts — do not merge them:

**Paragraph 1 — Global background:**
Who the brand is, headquarters, founding year if notable, what they manufacture, any verifiable market position. One factual paragraph. No superlatives without proof.

**Paragraph 2 — Singapore / Asia presence:**
How the brand is positioned in Singapore or Asia-Pacific. Local distribution, regional office, climate suitability, regulatory compliance. Why this brand works in Singapore's property context.

**Scope declaration — mandatory for all tiers:**
Use the exact language templates from Section 1 (brand tier table) above. For Tier 1 brands use the full-range or partial-range language. For Tier 2 brands use the conversion redirect language. For Tier 3 (GE Caddx) use the legacy maintenance language. Display as the styled callout box in the template.

### Additional scope language for Tier 1 partial-range brands:

**Partial range (e.g. Hikvision cameras only):**
"Securevision specifies [Brand] exclusively for [specific product type]. We do not integrate [Brand]'s [other categories] — those functions are handled by purpose-built specialists in our technology stack."

**Credential-only (HID Global):**
"Securevision specifies HID card readers and credentials as part of integrated access control systems. We pair HID readers with Suprema or ZKTeco controllers — combining HID's credential security with a full-featured access management platform."

## 4. SECTION 2 — WHY WE CARRY

3 reason cards. Engineering reasons only — not marketing claims. Each reason must explain specifically why Securevision chose this brand over alternatives in the same category.

**Good example (AJAX):** "Jeweller radio protocol achieves 2km open-air range with 7-year battery life — eliminating the wiring constraints that make alarm upgrades impractical in tenanted commercial spaces."

**Bad example:** "AJAX is the best alarm system available." ← never write this.

**After the 3 cards, add a SECURE™ Integration callout** — a full-width dark panel showing how this brand connects to Securevision's wider system architecture. This is what separates an integrator from a reseller. Use this HTML structure:

```html

  <div style="font-size:32px; flex-shrink:0;">🔗</div>
  <div>
    <p style="font-family:'Montserrat',sans-serif; font-size:11px; font-weight:700; text-transform:uppercase; letter-spacing:1.5px; color:rgba(255,255,255,0.5); margin-bottom:8px;">SECURE™ Integration</p>
    <p style="font-family:'Inter',sans-serif; font-size:15px; color:rgba(255,255,255,0.85); line-height:1.7; margin:0;">
      [1–2 sentences — specific integration point between this brand and another system in the Securevision stack]
    </p>
  </div>
</div>
```

Integration reference by brand:
- **Hikvision/Hanwha:** "Hikvision NVRs integrate directly with our EntryPass access control platform — an access denied event triggers an NVR bookmark, giving you a timestamped clip for every refused entry without manual searching."
- **AJAX:** "AJAX hubs connect to central monitoring via IP — an intrusion trigger simultaneously locks down EntryPass access points and pushes a live camera feed to the duty manager's phone."
- **FAAC:** "FAAC barriers integrate natively with GantryGo™ — an approved licence plate opens the barrier automatically, with a timestamped entry log pushed to the VESTA dashboard."
- **Suprema/ZKTeco:** "Suprema readers connect to elevator controllers for floor-level access — a resident's credential grants lift access only to their floor, with no rewiring of existing lift infrastructure."
- **Akuvox:** "Akuvox door stations integrate with VESTA™ — a visitor call rings the resident's mobile app; they can view the camera, speak, and release the door from anywhere."
- **RISCO/Paradox/DSC:** "Alarm panels connect to central monitoring via IP and GSM dual-path — a triggered zone sends an alert to the monitoring centre and simultaneously bookmarks the corresponding CCTV timestamp."
