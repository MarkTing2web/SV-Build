The Challenge: Modernising Dual Systems Without Rewiring

L'viv Residences, a 147-unit luxury condominium at Buona Vista, faced a dual challenge: upgrading both its building-wide intercom system and individual unit door access controls, all while working within the constraints of existing building infrastructure to avoid costly and disruptive renovations.

Legacy Intercom System Limitations

The existing intercom infrastructure offered only basic audio-only communication between visitors and residents. Video verification was impossible, leaving residents unable to visually confirm visitor identities before granting building access. The system lacked any mobile integration, meaning residents had to be physically present at their in-unit monitor to answer visitor calls — creating particular inconvenience for residents who were at work, travelling, or simply in different rooms when visitors arrived.

The Management Corporation (MCST) recognised that modern luxury residential developments increasingly offered mobile-first access control as a standard amenity. Residents expected the ability to answer visitor calls via smartphone, grant remote access to deliveries and service providers, and manage family member credentials through intuitive apps — capabilities the legacy system couldn't provide.

Unit-Level Access Control Ageing

Each of the 147 residential units relied on outdated fingerprint readers at individual unit doors. Over time, these first-generation biometric readers had degraded in performance — recognition accuracy declined, enrolment was cumbersome, and the single-factor authentication (fingerprint only) created access challenges when residents had wet hands, skin conditions, or injuries affecting fingerprint quality.

Residents frequently encountered lockout scenarios requiring property management intervention. Elderly owners struggled with the precise finger placement required for reliable recognition. Families needed multiple credential management for domestic helpers, adult children, or elderly parents, but the old system offered limited flexibility. The lack of backup authentication methods (cards, PINs, or mobile access) meant any biometric reader malfunction could lock residents out of their own homes.

Infrastructure Renovation Constraints

The most significant constraint was the building's existing wiring infrastructure. L'viv's original construction used 2-wire analog cabling for the intercom system — standard practice when the building was developed, but incompatible with modern IP-based video intercom systems that typically require Cat6 Ethernet cabling.

Running new Cat6 cables to 147 residential units would require extensive renovation work — removing ceiling panels or drilling through walls in occupied units, disrupting residents for weeks, and incurring substantial costs for materials and labour. The MCST needed a solution that could deliver modern IP video intercom functionality while leveraging the existing 2-wire infrastructure, avoiding the massive disruption and expense of complete rewiring.

Separate System Requirements

Adding complexity, the two systems served different security purposes and operated under different requirements. The video intercom system needed centralised management, guard portal integration, and mobile app connectivity — all requiring network infrastructure and cloud platform access. The unit door access control, however, functioned best as a standalone system — residents wanted reliable unit access even during network outages, and privacy concerns favoured keeping unit-level biometric data isolated from networked systems.

The MCST required a solution that could upgrade both systems simultaneously while respecting their different operational requirements, all within a constrained budget and timeline that minimised resident disruption.

Our Solution: Dual-System Architecture with 2-Wire Retrofit Innovation

Securevision designed a comprehensive dual-system upgrade that addressed both the building-wide video intercom requirements and individual unit access control needs, while innovatively leveraging existing 2-wire infrastructure to avoid costly rewiring across all 147 units.

System 1: Networked Video Intercom with 2-Wire Retrofit

The first major system component replaced the audio-only intercom with Akuvox's complete IP video intercom ecosystem, strategically designed to work over existing 2-wire cabling rather than requiring new Cat6 Ethernet installation throughout the building.

We deployed 9 Akuvox X915 video visitor panels at all entry points — the main gate plus lobby areas at each floor level (B1, B2, B3, and Level 1). These 8-inch touchscreen panels transformed the visitor experience from audio-only to high-definition video calls, enabling residents to visually verify visitor identities before granting building access. The X915 panels support multiple access modes: residents enrolled in the system can use face recognition for hands-free entry, tap RFID cards, enter PIN codes, or use the SmartPlus mobile app to generate temporary QR codes for expected visitors.

The 2-Wire Infrastructure Innovation

The breakthrough enabling modern IP video intercom without complete rewiring was the Akuvox NS-2 network switch — a specialised 2-wire-to-IP converter designed specifically for retrofitting older buildings. We installed 26 NS-2 units throughout the building's common area risers, with each unit supporting up to 6 connected devices.

The NS-2 serves dual functions: it converts the existing 2-wire analog cabling to carry IP network data, and simultaneously provides power to connected devices via the same 2-wire cable. This elegant solution meant we could deliver full IP video intercom capability to every unit without the massive expense and disruption of running new Cat6 cabling through 147 occupied residences.

Each of the 150 Akuvox C313 indoor monitors — installed in 147 residential units plus 3 common amenity areas (BBQ area and other shared facilities) — connects through the NS-2 infrastructure. Residents receive high-definition video calls when visitors arrive at any of the 9 entrance panels, viewing clear footage on their 7-inch touchscreen before deciding whether to grant access. The monitors integrate seamlessly with the SmartPlus mobile app, enabling residents to manage all access events, review visitor logs, and answer calls remotely even when away from home.

Centralised Guard Portal Management

We implemented the Akuvox VP-R49G guard portal at the security guard post, creating a centralised dashboard where guards monitor all 9 entrance panels simultaneously. The 7-inch touchscreen interface displays real-time visitor calls, allows manual access override when residents are unreachable, and maintains comprehensive logs of all visitor interactions.

When residents issue temporary digital keys through the SmartPlus app — for example, providing time-limited access to cleaning services, delivery personnel, or guests — the guard portal receives notifications showing which visitors have pre-authorised credentials. This automation dramatically reduces guard workload during high-traffic periods, as guards no longer need to manually verify, contact residents, and issue physical temporary cards for routine pre-approved visitors.

SmartPlus Mobile App Integration

The mobile application unifies all intercom functions into a single resident-facing interface accessible on both iOS and Android devices. Residents can answer visitor calls from anywhere — whether at home, in the office, or travelling abroad — viewing live video and granting or denying access with a single tap. The app supports time-limited temporary key generation with granular controls: residents specify exact validity windows like "weekdays 9 AM-12 PM for the next two weeks" for recurring cleaning services, or "one-time access today 2-4 PM" for furniture delivery.

All access events — successful entries, denied attempts, temporary key usage — automatically log in the app's activity history, providing residents complete audit trails for security and household management purposes. Families can manage multiple app instances, with up to 4 smartphones per household able to receive visitor notifications and control access.

System 2: Standalone Multi-Modal Unit Door Access

Completely separate from the networked intercom system, we replaced the ageing fingerprint readers at all 147 unit doors with ZKTeco MiniAC Plus multi-modal biometric readers. These compact readers provide five authentication methods, giving residents unprecedented flexibility in how they access their homes while maintaining standalone operation independent of network connectivity.

Face recognition provides the most seamless entry experience — residents simply approach their door, and the reader recognises them within 0.3 seconds, automatically unlocking without requiring any physical interaction. Palm vein recognition offers an alternative biometric mode that works even when residents are wearing masks, sunglasses, or have recently changed appearance (new hairstyle, beard, etc.), addressing limitations sometimes encountered with face-only systems.

For residents who prefer traditional credentials or need backup access methods during biometric reader calibration issues, the MiniAC Plus supports both RFID cards and PIN codes. This multi-modal redundancy ensures residents are never locked out due to temporary biometric recognition challenges caused by lighting conditions, facial coverings, or system variations.

Critically, these unit door readers operate completely standalone — all biometric templates, card data, and PIN codes are stored locally in each reader's memory. No network connection is required, meaning unit access remains fully functional even during network outages or internet disruptions. This standalone architecture also addresses privacy concerns, as individual unit biometric data never transmits over networks or stores in centralised databases.

Each reader supports up to 4,000 face templates, 1,500 palm templates, and 3,000 RFID cards, providing ample capacity for family members, domestic helpers, and temporary credentials without requiring complex management. The readers connect directly to each unit's electromagnetic lock, controlling access through standard door access control wiring completely independent of the video intercom network.
