Foundation of Protection

1. What is a Burglar Alarm System?

A burglar alarm system is a critical network of sensors and intelligence designed to detect unauthorised breach and trigger immediate deterrent responses. In the Singapore context, it serves as the primary psychological and physical line of defence for properties ranging from residential penthouses to high-security commercial vaults.

Evolution of Modern Security

Modern interfaces: Today's systems leverage biometrics and mobile cloud technology.

Burglars don't just steal material possessions—they steal your peace of mind. The decision to install a burglar alarm is one of the smartest investments you can make for your home or business.

Studies consistently show that homes without burglar alarms are three times more likely to be broken into than homes with visible alarm systems. The presence of alarm signage alone acts as a powerful deterrent, signaling to would-be intruders that your property is protected and not worth the risk.

Beyond deterrence, modern burglar alarms provide real-time alerts directly to your mobile phone, allowing you to respond immediately whether you're at home, at work, or traveling overseas. Integration with CCTV systems enables video verification—you can see what triggered the alarm and make informed decisions about whether to call the police.

Critical Strategic Capabilities

Today's burglar alarms go far beyond simple detection, serving as a unified security hub:

  • ✓ Mobile Ecosystem: Receive instant push notifications and arm/disarm your property from anywhere via encrypted cloud apps.
  • ✓ Visual Verification: Seamless integration with IP cameras allows for rapid verification of triggers, eliminating false alarm fatigue.
  • ✓ Adaptive Arming: Utilise distinct profiles for Day, Night, and Vacation modes to ensure appropriate protection levels at all times.
  • ✓ Building Automation: Link alarms to smart lighting and access control systems for a complete, reactive security ecosystem.
System Architecture

2. How Does a Burglar Alarm System Work?

An intrusion system relies on the seamless interplay between hardware detection and software logic. Understanding these core components is essential for designing a resilient security envelope.

The Six Core Components

A professional-grade alarm system is more than just a siren; it is a unified ecosystem of six essential hardware layers:

1 Control Panel (The Processor)

The control panel serves as the central intelligent hub of your security network. Typically installed in a secure internal location, it continuously monitors every sensor while managing user authentication and emergency communication protocols.

Modern panels feature multi-zone processing, allowing for precise identification of which area triggered an alarm. High-end units like the RISCO or Paradox systems can handle 50+ individual zones with independent arming logic.

2 User Interfaces (Keypads)

This is your primary point of interaction. Keypads allow for arming/disarming, status checks, and viewing alarm history. Modern options range from traditional tactile buttons to high-fidelity LCD touchscreens with intuitive, app-like navigation.

3 Detection Devices (Sensors)

Sensors are the ears and eyes of the system. They detect physical changes—like a door opening or heat-signature motion—and instantly report the violation to the control panel.

4 Notification (Sirens & Strobes)

When a breach occurs, sirens execute high-decibel auditory deterrence. External strobe units provide visual identification, helping emergency responders or security staff locate the specific property under threat.

5 Communication Modules

This layer ensures the alert leaves your property. Using IP (Internet), GSM (Mobile Data), or traditional landlines, the panel communicates triggers directly to your phone or a 24/7 Professional Monitoring Station.

6 Backup Power Unit

Security systems must survive power failures. Every panel contains a rechargeable 12V backup battery that automatically takes over during blackouts, providing hours of continued protection.

Sequential Operational Flow

A resilient system follows a strict logical sequence from detection to deterrence:

  1. 01 High-Fidelity Detection: A sensor (e.g., PIR or Magnetic Contact) is triggered. It instantly transmits an encrypted signal to its assigned zone on the control panel.
  2. 02 Logic Processing: The panel cross-references the arming state. If the zone is active (not bypassed), the processor initiates the alarm response logic.
  3. 03 Entry Delay Check: If triggered via a designated entry door, a countdown begins. Failure to input a valid PIN triggers full-scale activation. Instant zones trigger without a delay.
  4. 04 Primary Deterrence: Internal and external high-decibel sirens activate, while strobe units flash to provide visual identification of the breach.
  5. 05 Cloud Communication: The panel pushes alerts through primary (IP) and secondary (GSM/Cellular) data paths. Users receive biometric push notifications via cloud apps.
System Options

3. Types of Burglar Alarm Systems

Choosing the right system architecture depends on your property's lifecycle: whether you are currently in a renovation phase or looking for a rapid, non-invasive deployment.

Wired Systems (The Gold Standard)

Physical backbone: Wired systems provide life-long reliability without battery overhead.

Wired systems use physical cables to connect every sensor and keypad back to the main control panel. Cabling is typically concealed within ceilings, walls, or architectural conduits.

When to Choose Wired: If you're currently renovating or building a new property, a wired system is the superior choice. Since the walls are open or ceilings are accessible, running cables is highly cost-effective and provides the highest possible reliability without any battery maintenance for sensors.

Wired architectures remain the primary choice for commercial properties and high-security residential installations where long-term stability and physical signal paths are paramount.

Advantages

  • ✓ Zero Maintenance: Sensors never require battery replacements.
  • ✓ Highest Reliability: Impermeable to radio frequency interference or jamming.
  • ✓ Lower Hardware Cost: Wired sensors are typically 40% less expensive than wireless.
  • ✓ Longevity: Physical installations often last for 15-20+ years.

Considerations

  • ! Installation Scope: Requires invasive cabling throughout the building.
  • ! Aftermath: May require ceiling patching or visible trunking if done post-renovaion.
  • ! Static Placement: Moving sensors later requires new cable runs.

Wireless Systems (Modern Precision)

Clean Aesthetics: Minimalist wireless sensors blend into modern interior design.

Wireless systems use high-grade, battery-powered sensors that communicate via encrypted radio frequencies. These systems eliminate the need for data cabling, allowing for a "clean" installation that protects architectural finishes.

When to Choose Wireless: Wireless is the ideal solution for properties with completed renovations. Installation is rapid (typically under 6 hours) and entirely non-invasive. This makes them perfect for contemporary apartments, conservation projects, or rental units where drilling is restricted.

Securevision Engineering Verdict

Choose Wired if you are in the renovation phase—the upfront labour is offset by life-long reliability. Choose Wireless if your property is finished—you will benefit from state-of-the-art encryption with zero impact on your decor.

Detection Layer

4. Sensors & Detectors Explained

The efficacy of your alarm system is defined by its detection surface. We deploy a range of specialized sensors to ensure early warning across every architectural vulnerability.

Detection Components

Magnetic Contacts

The primary line of defense. These dual-part reed switches monitor the state of doors and windows, triggering instantly upon physical opening.

We specify Grade 2 high-power magnets to prevent frequent false alarms caused by loose door frames or thermal expansion in Singapore's tropical climate.

PIR Motion Sensors

Detect moving heat signatures. We utilize 'Pet-Immune' digital PIRs that distinguish between human intruders and small animals up to 25kg.

Modern PIRs feature advanced Digital Signal Processing (DSP) to filter out thermal turbulence from air-conditioning units or direct sunlight.

Vibration & Shock Sensors

Specifically designed for glass facades and wall-breaches. They detect the specific high-frequency frequency of glass breaking or physical impact before the intruder enters.

Internal Sounders

While external sirens alert the neighborhood, internal sounders emit piercing 110dB tones to disorientate the intruder and force them to vacate the premises immediately.

Advanced Detection Capabilities

External photo-beams create invisible 100m+ boundaries.

For high-sensitivity environments, we deploy specialized detection technologies:

  • ● Infrared Beams: Create invisible point-to-point boundaries across driveways or fence lines.
  • ● Dual-Tech Sensors: Combine PIR with Microwave frequency to virtually eliminate false alarms in unstable environments.
  • ● Smoke & Heat: Integration allows the burglar alarm to serve as a life-safety fire alert system.
System Design

5. Strategic System Design

Effective security is not about indiscriminate coverage; it is about protecting the right assets in the most logical sequence—the Layered Defense approach.

The Three Layers of Defense

We approach every property as a series of nested perimeters:

  • 01 | Perimeter: Securing doors, windows, and balconies with magnetic contacts and vibration shock sensors. This stops entry before it happens.
  • 02 | Interior: Deploying PIR motion sensors in common hallways and stairway landings to track movement if a perimeter breach occurs.
  • 03 | Specific Asset: Targeted sensors on safes, server racks, or master suite doors for high-value protection.

Property-Specific Planning

Offices & Retail

Focus on public entrances, inventory rooms, and receptionist panic buttons for employee safety. Automatic disarming via access control integration is standard.

Apartments & Condos

High-rise units focus on the main entrance and balcony sliding doors. Ideal for wireless Rapid Deployment kits that don't violate management rules.

Landed Estates

Comprehensive external perimeter protection using infrared beams and outdoor motion detectors to trigger alerts before an intruder reaches the physical building.

Six Factors That Determine Your Investment

Factor 1: Property Size & Layout
Larger properties require more sensors and complex wiring. Multi-story properties add installation scope due to cable runs between floors.

Factor 2: Number of Entry Points
Every door and accessible window adds to the hardware count. A landed home might have 20+ entry points compared to an apartment's 5.

Factor 3: Wired vs Wireless
Wired systems have lower sensor costs but higher labor. Wireless is faster to install but hardware is premium-priced.

Factor 4: Monitoring Requirements
Self-monitoring is free, while central monitoring (CAMS) adds annual professional fees.

How to Optimize Your Investment

  • Install During Renovation: Wired systems are significantly cheaper during this phase.
  • Right-Size Coverage: Focus on accessible entry points and use motion sensors as backup.
  • Start Essential: Expand with more sensors later as your budget allows.

Mobile Ecosystem Control

Smart ecosystem: Monitor zone status and arm/disarm via secure mobile cloud.

Modern alarm panels are entirely cloud-managed. Securevision provides a unified mobile app that serves as your remote command center:

  • ● One-Tap Arming: Secure your entire property with a single biometric-authenticated tap.
  • ● Real-Time Logs: See who armed the system and exactly at what time.
  • ● Fault Reporting: Receive instant low-battery or power-loss alerts directly to your phone.

Critical Arming Profiles

Systems are configured with adaptive modes to suit your presence at the property:

Away Mode

Full Perimeter + Interior

Used when the property is entirely vacant. All sensors, including internal motion detectors, are active.

Stay Mode

Perimeter Only

Used when you are home. Doors and windows are secured, but internal PIRs are bypassed so you can move freely.

Night Mode

Zoned Security

Typically secures the ground floor and external perimeters while allowing free movement on the second floor.

    10. Maintenance & Testing Protocols

    An alarm system provides a false sense of security unless it is rigorously tested. In Singapore's humid climate, sensor calibration and battery health are the two primary failure points.

    The Quarterly Walk-Test

    Maintain peak performance by executing these steps every 90 days:

    • 1. CAMS Notification: Inform your monitoring station before initiating a live test to avoid accidental police dispatch.
    • 2. Walk-Test Sequence: Systematic triggers for every door and window. Confirm sirens activate and strobe units flash correctly.
    • 3. Zone Mapping: Confirm that 'Front Door' on the keypad screen truly matches the physical door trigger.

    Critical Maintenance Schedule

    Monthly Signal Test

    Trigger one panic button or zone to confirm the communication module successfully sends alerts to your phone or the CMS monitoring station.

    Annual Professional Service

    Securevision engineers perform a deep-system audit, measuring battery impedance and updating panel firmware to patch critical security vulnerabilities.

    System Hardening

    11. Advanced Security Hardening Tips

    Multi-Path Alerting

    NEVER rely on a single communication path. Combine IP (Internet) with GSM (Mobile Data) so the system survives a cut fiber or phone line.

    Visual Verification

    Link your alarm with IP cameras. High-fidelity video clips pushed alongside alarm triggers eliminate false alarm uncertainty.

    TAMPER Resilience

    Ensure all external sirens and internal panels utilize '24H Tamper' zones that trigger an alarm if an intruder tries to pry them open.

    Encrypted Protocols

    Only use wireless systems that utilize AES-128 bit encryption to prevent signal jamming or replay attacks from sophisticated intruders.

    Pro Tip: Arm Consistently

    The best system provides zero protection if not armed. Use "Stay Mode" when home and "Night Mode" before bed to protect your perimeter while you sleep.

    Risk Mitigation

    12. Common Implementation Mistakes

    Failed security systems are rarely due to equipment failure—they are almost always caused by poor design or psychological shortcuts.

    The "Reactive" Procurement Trap

    80% of residential alarm inquiries occur in the 7 days following a local break-in. This leads to rushed, sub-optimal installations. Proactive hardening is 4x more cost-effective.

    Under-Securing Perimeter Points

    Focusing solely on front doors while ignoring side gates or ground-floor balconies. Professional burglars always target the point of least resistance/visibility.

    Inadequate Code Management

    Using shared codes for family, staff, and contractors. Modern systems allow for individual user IDs, providing a clear audit trail of who disarmed the system and when.

    The "Price-Only" Evaluation

    Choosing consumer-grade DIY kits for high-value properties. These lack the signal encryption and tamper-resilience required for meaningful protection.

    15. Frequently Asked Questions

    What is the typical lifespan of an alarm system?

    A professionally installed wired system has a core lifecycle of 10-12 years. Wireless sensors typically require battery replacement every 3-5 years. We recommend a core panel upgrade every decade to maintain compatibility with modern cloud services.

    Can I use my alarm if the Wi-Fi is down?

    Yes. All Securevision systems utilize local communication between sensors and the panel, independent of Wi-Fi. For external alerting, we configure dual-path communication (Internet + 4G/GSM) to ensure signals reach you even if your fiber line fails.

    Is there a monthly subscription fee?

    For self-monitoring via basic apps, there are typically no ongoing fees. For premium cloud features or 24/7 Professional CAMS Monitoring, a nominal monthly or annual fee applies to cover the cellular data and monitoring center overhead.
