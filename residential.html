System Architecture

What Systems Do Residential Homes Need?

Every home is different, but most residential properties benefit from the same four integrated security pillars. Here's how we design for landed homes, GCBs, semi-Ds, and terraces.

Surveillance & Detection

ESSENTIAL

Perimeter cameras to monitor gardens, driveways, and entry points. Motion sensors and alarms for intrusion detection. Thermal imaging for large properties. AI analytics to reduce false alarms from pets and foliage.

Typical components:
  • IP cameras (8-24 cameras typical)
  • Wireless alarm sensors
  • Perimeter beams for gardens
  • Video analytics (human/vehicle detection)
Learn More About Surveillance →

People Access Control

ESSENTIAL

Control who enters your home. Biometric fingerprint or face recognition for family members. Mobile app access for domestic helpers. Temporary PIN codes for contractors and visitors. Complete audit trail of every entry.

Typical components:
  • Digital door locks (3-5 doors typical)
  • Video intercoms
  • Mobile app credentials
  • Visitor management for helpers
Learn More About People Access →

Vehicle Access & Barriers

ESSENTIAL

Automated gates for driveways. License plate recognition (ANPR) for family vehicles. Video intercom for visitor verification before opening gates. Smart gate motors that integrate with alarm systems for security arming.

Typical components:
  • Automated sliding/swing gates
  • ANPR cameras for driveways
  • Video intercom at gate
  • Remote control via mobile app
Learn More About Vehicle Access →

Platform Integration

OPTIONAL

Connect all systems into one unified mobile app. View cameras, unlock doors, and open gates from anywhere. Automation rules like 'arm alarm when gate closes' or 'record video when doorbell rings.' Essential for estates with multiple buildings; optional for single homes.

Typical components:
  • Unified mobile dashboard
  • Cross-system automation
  • Remote monitoring
  • Cloud backup (optional)

Investment Guide: What Does Residential Security Cost?

Budget transparency is important. Here's what to expect based on your home's size and the systems you need. All prices include professional installation, training, and warranty.

2,000 – 3,500 sqft

Small to Medium Homes

Terraces • Corner Terraces • Small Semi-Ds

$5,000 – $15,000
  • 8-12 IP cameras (perimeter + entry points)
  • Wireless alarm system (10-15 sensors)
  • 1-2 automated gates (main gate + side gate)
  • Video intercom at main entrance
  • Mobile app control (basic features)
  • Professional installation + 1-year warranty

Starting point for most terrace homes. Scalable based on specific needs.

3,500 – 5,500 sqft

Medium to Large Homes

Large Semi-Ds • Detached • Small Bungalows

$10,000 – $30,000
  • 12-18 IP cameras (full perimeter coverage)
  • Hybrid alarm system (wired + wireless sensors)
  • 2-3 automated gates (main, side, rear)
  • Video intercoms at multiple points
  • Face recognition or biometric access
  • Platform integration (unified app control)
  • Professional installation + 2-year warranty

Most common configuration for semi-detached and detached homes.

5,500+ sqft

Large Homes & GCBs

Bungalows • GCBs • Estates

  • 18-30+ IP cameras (comprehensive coverage)
  • Advanced alarm with perimeter beams
  • 3-5 automated gates + barriers
  • Multiple video intercoms + lobby panels
  • Biometric access control (fingerprint/face)
  • Full platform integration + automation
  • Network infrastructure upgrades
  • Professional installation + 3-year warranty

Tailored engineering for large estates. Every GCB project is custom-designed.

What Affects the Final Cost?

Four factors determine your investment:

  1. Property size and perimeter length: Larger areas require more cabling and detection points.
  2. Number of entry points requiring coverage: Each gate and door adds to the system's hardware count.
  3. Integration complexity: Connecting new smart systems to existing legacy gate motors or wiring.
  4. Premium features: Advanced AI such as facial recognition or specialized intrusion sensors.
