Residential Strategy

Security Planned In.
Not Bolted On.

For Singapore homeowners building new or doing major A&A — the security decisions that need to be made before the walls close.

Most security problems in new homes trace back to a single decision point: security was briefed too late. Once walls are plastered and ceilings are closed, every cable run costs two to three times more and every camera position is a compromise. We have been the security specialist on Singapore new builds and A&A projects since 2006. Here is how we work with you from before construction starts.

Strategic Approach

Are You Building New or Renovating?

The engineering approach is fundamentally different. Tell us which situation you are in.

🏗️

New Build: From the Ground Up

A new build gives you the cleanest opportunity to design security into the structure from day one. Conduit routes, cable trays, power points, and device positions can all be specified before a single wall goes up. The result is a system that is invisible where it should be invisible, and effective where it counts. We work from your floor plans and provide a full pre-wiring schedule to your M&E contractor before construction starts.

🏡

A&A Renovation: Existing Home

An A&A renovation is more complex. Existing cable runs may be reusable or may need replacing. Existing gate motors, alarm panels, and intercoms may be worth retaining or may have reached end of life. We assess what is there, advise what is worth keeping, and plan new cable routes before walls are closed — so you are not chasing cables through finished plaster six months later.

Expert FAQ

Questions Every Homeowner Asks Us

We have answered all of these before. Here are the honest answers.

What systems do I actually need?

The Answer: It depends on your property size, number of entry points, and how you live. We map this for you after reviewing your floor plans — before you spend anything.

How do I choose technology that will not be outdated?

The Answer: We only specify brands with a proven Singapore distribution and support track record — Hikvision, Akuvox, AJAX, RISCO, FAAC. Nothing generic, nothing without local parts availability.

When should I bring security in?

The Answer: Before walls close. Ideally at the same time as your M&E contractor is engaged. If you have already started, call us now — there is usually still a window before plastering.

What is a realistic budget for my home size?

The Answer: For a landed terrace with full alarm, CCTV, intercom, and gate — expect $8,000 to $18,000 depending on specification. We provide a detailed breakdown after your site assessment.

My electrician says he will handle the wiring.

The Answer: Electricians wire for power. Security requires specific cable types and routing discipline that most electricians do not follow. We provide a schedule so they run the right cable, then we verify it.

How do I coordinate security with my ID?

The Answer: We work directly with your ID, architect, and contractor. We issue a pre-wiring schedule and fit our timeline around your handover date — not the other way around.

Engineering Workflow

How We Work With Your Build

Four stages, coordinated with your construction timeline.

1

Planning Session

We review floor plans, discuss lifestyle and security priorities, and provide a preliminary system recommendation with budget range. This happens before construction starts and costs you nothing.

2

Pre-Wiring Package

We produce a detailed pre-wiring schedule: cable specifications, conduit routes, device locations, and power requirement. This goes directly to your M&E contractor and eliminates guesswork on site.

3

Rough-In Check

We visit site before plastering to verify the electrician's cable runs against our schedule. Problems caught here are fixed in minutes. The same problems found after handover cost days.

4

Final Install & Training

We install all equipment, commission and test every system, and conduct a full handover training with you and your household. You leave knowing how to use everything — not hoping the manual is in a drawer.

The Infrastructure

What Goes Into a Well-Planned System

Most new builds need some combination of these four layers.

📹

Surveillance

A perimeter camera system covering the driveway, gate, and passages. Positioned during construction so cables are concealed. AI distingue between people and animals — useful in landed areas with wildlife.

🚨

Alarm System

Wired systems are significantly cleaner than wireless retrofits. Sensors on every external door and motion detection on each floor. Pre-wiring means detectors are flush-mounted and cables are invisible.

📞

Intercom and Gate

Video intercom at the main gate, connected to indoor monitors on each floor and a smartphone app. Gate motor automation integrated with the intercom so the gate can be released from anywhere.

🔐

Door Access

Digital locks on all main access points. Biometric or card access configured so that each person has their own credential — not a shared key that cannot be revoked if lost or if a contractor leaves.
