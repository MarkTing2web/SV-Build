The Philosophy

Access Control: Authorisation by Identity

Access control isn't about locking doors — it's about answering three fundamental questions:

Who are they?

Separating permanent users (residents, tenants) from temporary visitors requiring one-time approval.

What credential?

Matching patterns: cards, tokens, mobile phones, and biometric face recognition.

How to prove?

Generating an audit trail showing exactly who entered, when, and who granted them authorisation.

Two Distinct Groups: How We Design

Our design approach architects separate paths based on the group's relationship with the property.

👥 Permanent Users

Daily Access with Ongoing Credentials

Who They Are

  • • Employees (office staff, retail workers)
  • • Residents & homeowners (condo, landed)
  • • Tenants (commercial, industrial)
  • • Family members (authorised household)

Access Strategies

  • 🪪 RFID & Fobs: Low-friction, durable, and cost-effective.
  • 🩺 Biometric (Face/Palm): Unshareable security.
  • 📱 Mobile NFC/App: Convenient smartphone credentials.

👋 Temporary Users

Supervised or Time-Limited Entry

The Tiers

  • • Tier 1: Casual visitors & delivery drivers
  • • Tier 2: Pre-registered meeting guests
  • • Tier 3: Regular contractors (cleaners, interns)
  • • Tier 4: Ad-hoc food/parcel couriers

Access Strategies

  • 📞 Video Intercom: Visual verification.
  • 🏷️ Temp QR Codes: Time-limited self-service.
  • 💾 Temp Cards: Credentials that auto-expire.

This isn't about installing card readers everywhere. It's about architecting credential strategies — matching the right credential type to each user group's access pattern and security requirements.

How It Works

Why Identity-Based Access Matters: Three Scenarios

Conventional access methods often leave significant security gaps. Identity-based authorisation is designed to eliminate them by ensuring accountability for every entry.

Vulnerability: High Risk

Shared Access

  • • Office uses "1234" PIN for all staff
  • • Ex-employees still know the code
  • • Code shared with unauthorised cleaners
  • • No record of who entered when

Security Gap: PIN/key sharing leads to complete loss of accountability.

Vulnerability: Blind Spot

Unverified Visitors

  • • Audio-only barrier — "I'm for Unit 05"
  • • Resident hears a voice, releases lock
  • • Visitor has no ID record in system
  • • No photo log of entry or exit

Security Gap: Audio verification is easily faked by intruders.

Status: Secured

Verified Authorisation

  • • Face scanned or video-verified call
  • • Visitor's photo linked to entry log
  • • Resident sees visitor before unlock
  • • Instant alerts for unauthorised events

Outcome: 100% accountability with a visual and digital audit trail.

This is why we design for identity. Each person gets credentials matched to their pattern, visitors require visual verification, and every entry is logged.

Credentials

Choosing the Right Credential Type

Every credential type has trade-offs between security, convenience, and cost. Many properties use multi-modal credentials — combining two or more types for different user groups or security zones.

Credential Type Security Convenience Cost Can Be Shared? Can Be Lost?
PIN Code Low-Medium Medium Very Low ✅ Yes ❌ No
RFID Card Medium High Low ✅ Yes ✅ Yes
Fingerprint High Medium Medium ❌ No ❌ No
Face Recognition High Very High High ❌ No ❌ No
Mobile NFC/BLE Med-High High Medium ⚠️ Partially ✅ Yes

Multi-Modal Credential Strategies

Zone-Based Multi-Modal

Different credentials for different security zones. RFID for lobbies (low friction), but Face Recognition + PIN for the server room (high security).

Used By: Commercial offices, industrial facilities

User-Type Multi-Modal

Employees use biometrics (unshareable), visitors use temporary app-based PINs (auto-expire), and cleaners use revocable RFID cards.

Used By: Gated condos, co-working spaces

Time-Based Multi-Modal

RFID only during office hours (high throughput). After 6 PM, dual-factor (Card + PIN) is required to ensure stricter identity checks.

Used By: Commercial buildings, high-value retail

Integration: Access + Surveillance for Visual Verification

In developments where Access Control + CCTV integrate, guards can visually confirm identity against the presented credential in real-time.

Why This Matters

  • 🛡️ Prevents Credential Sharing: Cross-check face vs card data.
  • 🏛️ Visual Proof for Audit: Photo logs linked to every entry.
  • 🔎 Suspicious Pattern Detection: Catch unauthorised "tailgaters".
