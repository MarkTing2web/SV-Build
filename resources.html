Quick Answers

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions answered by our team. Can’t find what you need? Contact us directly.

CCTV

What's the difference between IP cameras and analog CCTV?

IP cameras transmit digital video over network cables and offer higher resolution (up to 4K), remote access, and advanced features like motion detection and analytics. Analog cameras use coaxial cables and offer lower resolution but are more cost-effective for basic monitoring needs. Most modern installations use IP cameras for flexibility and image quality.

Access Control

Can I use my smartphone to unlock doors with access control?

Yes, modern access control systems support mobile credentials via Bluetooth or NFC. You can use your smartphone as a digital key, eliminating the need for physical cards. This is particularly useful for visitor management and temporary access grants. The system can also send you notifications when doors are accessed.

Alarm Systems

How do I prevent false alarms from my security system?

False alarms are typically caused by improper sensor placement, pets triggering motion detectors, or environmental factors. Use pet-immune sensors, ensure proper installation away from air vents and windows, and train all users on proper arming/disarming procedures. Regular maintenance and sensor testing also significantly reduce false alarms.

CCTV

How long does CCTV footage get stored?

Storage duration depends on your hard drive capacity, camera resolution, and recording settings. Typical installations store 30-90 days of footage. Using motion-activated recording instead of continuous recording can extend storage time. Cloud storage options are also available for longer retention and offsite backup.

Intercom

Can video intercom systems integrate with my existing access control?

Yes, most modern IP-based intercom systems can integrate with access control platforms. This allows you to see visitors on your intercom screen and grant access remotely using the same credentials as your access control system. Integration creates a seamless visitor management experience.

CCTV

Do I need internet for my CCTV system to work?

No, CCTV systems work independently of internet connectivity. Cameras record to local storage (NVR/DVR) without internet. However, internet is required for remote viewing on mobile apps and cloud backup features. For maximum reliability, we recommend local storage with optional cloud backup.
