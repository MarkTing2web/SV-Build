The Philosophy

Layered Detection: The Engineering Approach

Detection isn't about buying the most cameras or the loudest alarms. It's about understanding three core engineering questions before a single device is specified.

🛡️

1. What are you protecting?

Protecting people and shared assets in a condo estate requires a different layer than protecting high-value inventory in an industrial yard.

🔍

2. What vulnerabilities exist?

We map blind spots, poor lighting zones, and perimeter entry points to ensure total coverage without hardware waste.

⚠️

3. How critical is each zone?

A public lobby needs situational awareness; a server room needs dual-verification. We designate criticality for every zone.

Our surveillance design approach layers two detection systems — alarms and cameras — to create defence in depth. While cameras provide visual evidence, alarms provide immediate proactive alerts. Together, they eliminate the delay between an incident and a response.

This isn't about forcing one platform. It's about architecting the right detection layers — whether integrated through Ajax, Risco, Hikvision, or kept separate — to match your threat profile and operational needs.

How It Works

Why Detection Layers Matter

Every detection method has a blind spot:

  • • Alarms alone tell you something triggered — but not what. False alarms waste response time. Real threats might be dismissed as another false alert.
  • • Cameras alone show you exactly what’s happening — but only if someone's watching that exact feed at that exact moment. Threats go unnoticed until it's too late.
  • • Layered detection eliminates both blind spots. The alarm triggers instant notification. The camera provides immediate visual verification. Your guard gets both: real-time alert AND video proof — in under 5 seconds.

Here's the difference in practice:

❌ Alarm Only

Scenario:

  • • Motion sensor triggers at 2 AM
  • • Guard gets alert: "Zone 3 breach"
  • • No visual confirmation
  • • Guard must physically investigate (3-5 mins)
  • • Cause unknown (animal, wind, intruder)

Result:

Wasted response time, high false alarm risk.

⚠️ Camera Only

Scenario:

  • • Intruder crosses perimeter at 2 AM
  • • No immediate alert (passive recording)
  • • Guard notices 10 mins later by chance
  • • Intruder already inside the building
  • • Investigation happens too late

Result:

Delayed detection, reactive threat response.

✅ Layered Detection

Scenario:

  • • Motion sensor triggers at 2 AM
  • • Alarm pushes live video within 1 second
  • • AI logic confirms: "Human Detected"
  • • Visual verification in under 5 seconds
  • • Police/Guard dispatched immediately

Result:

Instant detection, verification, and decision.

This is why we design in layers. Each system covers the weakness of the other.

Core Systems

The Two Detection Systems

We architect our surveillance solutions using two distinct but complementary detection layers: visual intelligence and physical intrusion sensing.

📹 CCTV & AI Video Analytics

Visual Intelligence Layer

Modern IP cameras aren't just recording devices — they're intelligent detection systems with built-in AI capabilities:

Built-in AI Features:

  • • Line Crossing Detection — Alert when someone crosses a virtual boundary
  • • Intrusion Zone Detection — Monitor specific areas (doorways, restricted zones)
  • • Motion Detection — Trigger recording only when movement occurs
  • • Human/Vehicle Classification — Reduce false alarms from pets or cars
  • • Loitering Detection — Alert when someone stays in an area too long
  • • Face Detection — Count people, identify known/unknown faces

When to Use Cameras:

  • ✓ High-visibility deterrence
  • ✓ Evidence capture (HD footage for investigations)
  • ✓ Remote monitoring (check property from anywhere)
  • ✓ Blind spot coverage
  • ✓ Post-incident investigation

Platform Options:

  • • Hikvision Hik-Connect app — Monitor cameras & receive AI alerts
  • • Integration capable — Works with Ajax, Risco, third-party VMS
  • • Can operate standalone or integrated

🔔 Burglar Alarms & Intrusion Sensors

Instant Detection Layer

Alarms provide the fastest intrusion detection — triggering within milliseconds of a breach, long before someone appears on camera.

Sensor Types:

  • • PIR Motion Detectors — Detect body heat movement in rooms
  • • Dual-Technology Sensors — PIR + microwave for fewer false alarms
  • • Door/Window Contacts — Trigger when entry points open
  • • Glass Break Sensors — Detect shattering sound frequency
  • • Perimeter Beams — Invisible infrared barriers for gates/fences
  • • Vibration Sensors — Detect fence cutting or wall drilling

When to Use Alarms:

  • ✓ Perimeter security (detect before they reach building)
  • ✓ Entry point monitoring (doors, windows, loading bays)
  • ✓ After-hours protection
  • ✓ Critical zone protection (server rooms, restricted areas)
  • ✓ Instant alert requirement

Platform Options with Camera Integration:

  • • Ajax Systems app — Integrates Ajax alarms with Ajax/IP cameras
  • • Risco Cloud app — Integrates Risco alarms with camera verification
  • • Can trigger Hikvision cameras when alarm activates
  • • Standalone alarm systems also available

Integration

When Alarms & Cameras Work As One

The power of layered detection multiplies when alarms and cameras communicate. Automated triggers eliminate the reliance on manual monitoring.

How Integration Works:

1

Alarm sensor detects intrusion — a door opens or motion is detected in a restricted zone.

2

System instantly triggers camera — associated cameras begin high-bitrate recording immediately.

3

Camera auto-focuses — PTZ cameras can be programmed to swing to the triggered zone automatically.

4

AI analytics verify threat — Deep learning algorithms confirm if the trigger was human, vehicle, or animal.

5

Guard receives live feed — Push notifications include a direct link to the real-time video event.

6

Decision made in seconds — Informed confirmation ensures the right response is dispatched immediately.

Unified Platform

Examples:

  • • Ajax ecosystem (Alarms + Cameras)
  • • Risco Cloud (Integrated app)
  • • Hikvision AX PRO (Unified system)

Best for:

New installations, desire for single-app control

Cross-Platform

Examples:

  • • Ajax alarms trigger Hikvision cameras
  • • Risco app integrates 3rd-party IP cams
  • • VMS platform unifying multiple brands

Best for:

Existing mixed systems, brand preferences

Separate Systems

Examples:

  • • Alarms on Ajax/Risco app
  • • Cameras on Hikvision app
  • • Both function independently

Best for:

Simple setups, budget constraints

Important Note:

Integration is valuable but not mandatory. A property with separate alarm and camera apps still benefits from layered detection — you just manage them separately. Integration adds convenience and automation, but both systems work effectively on their own.

Beyond Security

Detection Systems for Safety, Not Just Security

The same surveillance and detection technologies that protect against intrusions can also safeguard vulnerable individuals.

Main Use Case

📹 Elderly Care & Assisted Living

Monitoring seniors at home or in care facilities

Detection Layers:

  • • Motion sensors (bedrooms, bathrooms, hallways)
  • • Camera AI analytics (fall detection, zone monitoring)
  • • Non-movement alerts (no motion detected for X hours)
  • • Wandering detection (person exits safe zone)

Privacy Considerations:

  • • Common area cameras, never in bathrooms
  • • AI detects falls without storing visual data
  • • Alert-only systems (notification without recording)

How It Works:

1

Activity Mapping: Sensors confirm regular activity patterns (e.g., kitchen use at 8 AM).

2

AI Assessment: Cameras with edge-AI detect unusual positions (human down on floor).

3

Non-Movement Alert: System triggers if no motion detected for fixed periods (e.g., 4 hours).

4

Instant Notification: Family or caregivers receive a priority alert on their mobile devices.

"This isn't surveillance — it's care. The same detection technology that secures buildings can also provide peace of mind for families with elderly loved ones."
