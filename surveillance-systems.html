The Philosophy

Layered Detection: The Engineering Approach

Detection isn't about buying the most cameras or the loudest alarms. It's about understanding three core engineering questions before a single device is specified.

1. What are you protecting?

Protecting people and shared assets in a condo estate requires a different layer than protecting high-value inventory in an industrial yard.

2. What vulnerabilities exist?

We map blind spots, poor lighting zones, and perimeter entry points to ensure total coverage without hardware waste.

3. How critical is each zone?

A public lobby needs situational awareness; a server room needs dual-verification. We designate criticality for every zone.

Our surveillance design approach layers two detection systems — alarms and cameras — to create defence in depth. While cameras provide visual evidence, alarms provide immediate proactive alerts. Together, they eliminate the delay between an incident and a response.

This isn't about forcing one platform. It's about architecting the right detection layers — whether integrated through Ajax, Risco, Hikvision, or kept separate — to match your threat profile and operational needs.

How It Works

Why Detection Layers Matter

Every detection method has a blind spot:

  • Alarms alone tell you something triggered — but not what. False alarms waste response time, and real threats might be dismissed.
  • Cameras alone show exactly what’s happening — but only if someone is watching that exact feed at the moment an event occurs.
  • Layered detection eliminates both blind spots. The alarm triggers an instant persistent alert, while the camera provides the visual truth.

Here's the difference in practice:

❌ Alarm Only

Scenario:

  • • Motion sensor triggers at 2 AM
  • • Guard gets alert: "Zone 3 breach"
  • • No visual confirmation
  • • Guard must physically investigate (3-5 mins)
  • • Cause unknown (animal, wind, intruder)

Result:

Wasted response time, high false alarm risk.

⚠️ Camera Only

Scenario:

  • • Intruder crosses perimeter at 2 AM
  • • No immediate alert (passive recording)
  • • Guard notices 10 mins later by chance
  • • Intruder already inside the building
  • • Investigation happens too late

Result:

Delayed detection, reactive threat response.

✅ Layered Detection

Scenario:

  • • Motion sensor triggers at 2 AM
  • • Alarm pushes live video within 1 second
  • • AI logic confirms: "Human Detected"
  • • Visual verification in under 5 seconds
  • • Police/Guard dispatched immediately

Result:

Instant detection, verification, and decision.

This is why we design in layers. Each system covers the blind spot of the other, ensuring no threat goes unverified.

Core Systems

The Two Detection Systems

We architect our surveillance solutions using two distinct but complementary detection layers: visual intelligence and physical intrusion sensing.

CCTV & AI Video Analytics

Visual Intelligence Layer

Modern IP cameras aren't just recording devices — they're intelligent detection systems with built-in AI capabilities.

Built-in AI Features:

  • • Line Crossing Detection — Alert when someone crosses a virtual boundary
  • • Intrusion Zone Detection — Monitor gateways or restricted zones
  • • Human/Vehicle Classification — Reduce false alarms by up to 90%
  • • Loitering Detection — Alert when subjects linger in specified areas
  • • Face & LPR — Identify persons of interest or license plates

When to Use Cameras:

  • ✓ High-value visual areas (Lobbies, Corridors)
  • ✓ Identification of persons/vulnerabilities
  • ✓ Real-time visual verification of alerts

Alarms & Physical Sensors

Physical Intrusion Layer

Sensors provide the earliest possible detection of a physical breach, often triggering before an intruder even enters.

Detection Sensor Types:

  • • Vibration Sensors — Detect window shattering or wall-scale
  • • Magnetic Contacts — Detect opening of doors or hatches
  • • PIR Motion Sensors — Detect heat movement in restricted halls
  • • Beam Breaks — Create invisible perimeters around gardens
  • • Glass Break — Detect acoustic frequency of breaking glass

When to Use Sensors:

  • ✓ Total darkness where cameras have lower ranges
  • ✓ Immediate notification without millisecond delay
  • ✓ Early-warning detection of fence breaches

Integration

When Alarms & Cameras Work As One

The power of layered detection multiplies when alarms and cameras communicate. Automated triggers eliminate the reliance on manual monitoring.

VESTA™ Powered

How Integration Works:

1

Alarm sensor detects intrusion — a door opens or motion is detected.

2

System instantly triggers camera — cameras begin high-bitrate recording immediately.

3

Decision made in seconds — Informed confirmation ensures the right response is dispatched.

Separate Systems

Examples:

  • • Alarms on Ajax/Risco app
  • • Cameras on Hikvision app
  • • Both function independently

Best for:

Simple setups, budget constraints

Important Note:

Integration is valuable but not mandatory. A property with separate alarm and camera apps still benefits from layered detection — you just manage them separately. Integration adds convenience and automation, but both systems work effectively on their own.

Scalability

Layered Detection Scaled to Your Property

The same design philosophy applies across all property sizes — only the scale and complexity change.

Small Properties

Homes, Small Offices, & Clinics

Typical Setup:

  • • 4-8 IP cameras (entry + interior)
  • • Door/window contacts (all entries)
  • • 1-2 motion detectors in main zones
  • • Mobile app monitoring

Design Focus:

  • • Entry point coverage (front/back/windows)
  • • Interior verification (hallways/rooms)
  • • Simple arming/disarming logic

Platform Approach:

Separate apps (Ajax/Hikvision) or single-app integration (convenience-driven).

Investment Range:

$3,000 — $12,000

Medium Properties

Condos, Retail, & Offices

Typical Setup:

  • • 15-40 IP cameras (car park/perimeter)
  • • Perimeter beams + motion sensors
  • • Access control audit integration
  • • Guard monitoring station

Design Focus:

  • • Zoned criticality (public vs restricted)
  • • Visual verification of all alarm zones
  • • Multi-user audit trails

Platform Approach:

Unified platform or VMS recommended for faster response.

Investment Range:

$15,000 — $60,000

Large Properties

Industrial, Campus, & Warehouses

Typical Setup:

  • • 50-200+ IP cameras (mass coverage)
  • • Thermal perimeter detection + AI
  • • Central Command Station
  • • Site-wide wireless links

Design Focus:

  • • Proactive perimeter early-warning
  • • Autonomous AI threat filtering
  • • Multi-system coordination (LPR/Face)

Platform Approach:

Unified VMS platform critical for cross-system coordination.

Investment Range:

$80,000 — $300,000+

Beyond Security

Detection Systems for Safety, Not Just Security

The same surveillance and detection technologies that protect against intrusions can also safeguard vulnerable individuals.

Main Use Case

📹 Elderly Care & Assisted Living

Monitoring seniors at home or in care facilities

Detection Layers:

  • • Motion sensors (bedrooms, bathrooms, hallways)
  • • Camera AI analytics (fall detection, zone monitoring)
  • • Non-movement alerts (no motion detected for X hours)
  • • Wandering detection (person exits safe zone)

How It Works:

1

Activity Mapping: Sensors confirm regular activity patterns.

2

AI Assessment: Cameras with edge-AI detect unusual positions (human down on floor).

Privacy Considerations:

  • • AI detects falls without storing visual data
  • • Alert-only systems (notification without recording)

"This isn't surveillance — it's care. The same detection technology that secures buildings can also provide peace of mind for families with elderly loved ones."

Other Safety Applications

Pool Area Monitoring

Detect if a child enters the pool perimeter unsupervised using AI virtual fences.

Lone Worker Safety

Detect if a staff member is "man-down" in warehouse or restricted areas via AI analytics.

Fire/Smoke Integration

Cameras visually verify the source of fire alarms, reducing false facility evacuations.
