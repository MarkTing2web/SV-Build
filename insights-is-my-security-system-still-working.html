How Do You Know If Your Current Security System Is Still Working?

Most security systems appear to be working right up until the moment they are not. Here is how to check — without waiting for the wrong kind of proof.

Wee Meng Ler
Founder & CEO · Securevision · 2026 · 6 min read
  • Home
  • Insights
  • Security Planning
  • System Health Checks

1. The problem with systems that "seem fine"

Confidence in a silent system is often false. The backup battery may have degraded, the hard disk could have corrupted sectors, or the communication module might have quietly failed months ago. None of these announce themselves on the keypad until you actually need the system.

2. Checking your burglar alarm

  • Keypad Indicators: Check for a fault symbol or beep. Read the fault menu — don't just silence it.
  • Alarm History: Look for unexpected triggers or regular communication errors.
  • Communication Path: Trigger a test alarm — alerting your monitoring centre first — to confirm notifications reach your app or the monitoring desk.
  • Backup Battery: If it's over 3 years old, replace it regardless of fault lights.
  • The Walk-test: Arm the system and trigger every door and motion sensor to verify registration. Perform this annually.

3. Checking your CCTV system

  • Online Status: Ensure every camera channel shows a live feed. Black screens are dead recording channels.
  • Playback Check: Verify that footage from yesterday or earlier is actually stored and plays back smoothly. Corridor corruption is frequent on old disks.
  • Recording Retention: Know how many days you actually have, not what you "expected." Higher resolution may have shortened your retention window.

Verification of stored footage via the NVR's playback timeline.

4. Checking your video intercom

Press the call button. Verify the indoor monitor rings, the image is clear, and two-way audio works. For wired systems, blurry images often indicate camera lens degradation indicating it's at end of life.

5. The annual check — a simple schedule

KEY POINT

The one check most homeowners never do is the playback test. Cameras showing live doesn't guarantee the footage is searchable when you need evidence. Test it once a year.

6. Signs that something is wrong

Keypad beeping, "device offline" alerts, tampered siren box lights, and blurry intercom feeds are all signs your system needs immediate assessment.

We don't recommend service plans as a revenue exercise, but because these checks are routinely ignored. If you have the discipline, you can do this yourself. If not, professional eyes once a year are worth the fraction of the cost discovered during a real incident.
