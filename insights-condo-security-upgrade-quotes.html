How Do I Get Quotes for an AGM-Approved Security Upgrade?

An AGM resolution approves the budget and the intent. It does not tell you how to run a proper tender, what to put in a specification brief, or how to avoid ending up with three quotes that are impossible to compare.

Wee Meng Ler Founder & CEO · April 2, 2026 · 6 min read
  • Home
  • Insights
  • Singapore Industry
  • Getting Security Quotes

1. The problem with most condo security tenders

The most common approach to getting security upgrade quotes in Singapore condominiums is to invite three contractors to visit the site, show them around, and ask them to submit proposals. The results are three documents with different scopes, different brands, different camera counts, and different approaches to the upgrade — none of which can be compared directly.

One contractor proposes sixteen cameras at $95,000. Another proposes twenty-four cameras at $140,000. The third proposes twelve cameras at $72,000 with a managed service component. The committee is now trying to choose between three genuinely different proposals rather than three prices for the same thing.

This is not the contractors' fault. They were not given a specification. They proposed what they thought was appropriate. The way to get comparable, useful quotes is to define what you want before you ask anyone to price it.

2. Step 1 — get an independent assessment first

Before you approach any integrator for a quotation, commission an independent technical assessment of your existing system and your security requirements. This can be done by an independent security consultant, or by an integrator who is explicitly engaged for assessment only.

The assessment produces: a documentation of existing systems and their condition, identification of coverage gaps and failing components, and a scope of works document that can be issued to all tendering contractors.

When every contractor is quoting against the same specification — the same camera count, the same technical requirements — their quotes become genuinely comparable. You are comparing prices for the same thing, not proposals for different things.

3. Step 2 — prepare the specification document

The specification document is the foundation of a useful tender. It should cover:

  • Scope of works. Which systems are being replaced — CCTV, access control, intercom.
  • Technical specifications. Minimum camera resolution, compression standard (H.265 mandatory), storage configuration (RAID for larger developments).
  • Brand requirements. If the MCST or MA has a preference for specific brands based on consistency.
  • Compliance requirements. Police Licence requirement (mandatory) and BCA registration.
  • Submission format. Itemised bill of quantities, project timeline, and installation methodology.
A managing agent reviewing a printed tender specification, marking critical compliance requirements before issuing to contractors.

4. Step 3 — select and approach the contractors

Invite three to five contractors. Fewer than three gives you insufficient price comparison. More than five creates administrative overhead. Check for Police Licences (mandatory under Singapore law) and their specific track record in condominium projects.

TENDER TIP

Conduct a single site briefing where all contractors walk the site together. This ensures all parties working from the same information and have seen the same conditions simultaneously.

5. Step 4 — evaluate the submissions

When submissions arrive, evaluate them against the specification, not against each other's proposed scope. Price alone is not a sufficient criterion. A submission significantly below the others usually means scope has been cut — items omitted or specifications downgraded.

6. Step 5 — appoint and contract

Formalise the appointment with a written contract that covers scope, budget, project timeline with milestones, payment schedule, and warranty terms. Do not proceed on a verbal agreement or a basic purchase order alone.

We participate in both types of tender — open tenders with a specification, and exploratory quotations. We perform better in the first type, and so do our clients. A clear specification produces a cleaner proposal and a fairer comparison. If you need help preparing a specification, we can assist with that assessment.
