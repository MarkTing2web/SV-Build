The Philosophy

Vehicle Access: Authorisation by Purpose

Vehicle access control isn't about installing boom gates — it's about answering three fundamental questions:

Who is the driver?

Separating authorised users (tenants, employees, fleet) from temporary visitors requiring one-time approval.

What credential does the vehicle carry?

Matching technology: license plates (ANPR), IU tags, long-range RFID, or guard-verified intercom entry.

How do we prove and log it?

Creating audit trails showing vehicle plate, timestamp, entry/exit records, and who authorised access.

Two Distinct User Groups: How We Design

Our design approach architects separate authorisation paths based on the vehicle's relationship with the property.

🚗

Authorised Vehicles

Daily Access with Ongoing Credentials

Who They Are

  • Residents & homeowners (registered vehicles)
  • Tenants (commercial, industrial lease holders)
  • Employees (corporate fleet, staff parking)
  • Company vehicles (logistics, service fleet)

Access Strategies

  • 🚘 ANPR: License plate recognition, touchless and fast.
  • 📡 IU Tags (Singapore): Built-in ERP readers.
  • 📻 UHF Long-Range: 5-10m read distance, no stopping.
  • 🏷️ RFID Stickers: Windshield-mount credentials.
🚕

Temporary Vehicles

Supervised or Time-Limited Entry

The Tiers

  • Tier 1: Casual visitors (friends, family)
  • Tier 2: Contractors (maintenance, cleaners)
  • Tier 3: Delivery vehicles (couriers, food)
  • Tier 4: Ride-hailing (Grab, taxi)

Access Strategies

  • 📞 Video Intercom: Visual verification by guard.
  • 📋 Pre-Registration: Host generates plate approval.
  • 🏷️ Temp RFID: Expiring tags.
  • 👮 Guard Override: Manual dashboard release.

"This isn't about installing boom gates everywhere. It's about architecting credential strategies — matching the right detection technology to each vehicle type's entry pattern and security requirements."

How It Works

Why Vehicle Credential Systems Matter

Manual gate control and audio-only intercoms create significant security gaps. Credential-based vehicle authorisation eliminates them.

Vulnerability: High Risk

Shared Remotes

Office gives one remote to all staff. Ex-employees still have copies. No log of who entered when — leading to zero accountability.

Vulnerability: Blind Entry

Audio-Only Intercom

Guard hears voice, opens barrier. No visual of vehicle or driver. No plate number logged. Easily faked by unauthorised vehicles.

Solution: Full Accountability

ANPR + Video Intercom

ANPR reads plate automatically. Unknown plates trigger video intercom. Guard sees vehicle + driver via camera. System logs all events.

Credential Technologies

How Singapore Vehicles Are Identified

We deploy six primary credential technologies, each suited to different vehicle types, traffic volumes, and security requirements.

ANPR (License Plate Recognition)

AI-powered cameras read Singapore plates at 5-50 km/h. Perfect for condominiums and commercial car parks.

Typical Cost
$3,000 – $8,000 per lane

IU Tag Readers (ERP System)

Reads In-Vehicle Unit (IU) tags used for ERP tolls. Best for season parking and LTA-aligned gantry systems.

Typical Cost
$4,000 – $10,000 per lane

UHF Long-Range RFID

Windshield-mounted tags read at 5-10 meters. High-traffic reliability for industrial and corporate campuses.

Typical Cost
$2,500 – $6,000 per lane

RFID Stickers (Passive Tags)

Passive stickers read at 1-3 meters. Very low cost. Best for smaller landed properties and temporary contractors.

Typical Cost
$1,500 – $3,500 per gate

Video Intercom (Guard-Verified)

Visual verification before release. Essential backup for delivery lanes and visitor lanes in mixed-use properties.

Typical Cost
$2,000 – $5,000 per station

Pre-Registration (VESTA™)

Residents pre-register visitor plates via app. Whitelists plate for touchless guest entry at specific times.

Typical Cost
Included in VESTA™ Platform

Physical Barriers

Car Park Barriers vs Auto Gates

The credential technology identifies. The barrier mechanism controls physical access. We deploy types based on duty cycle and traffic volume.

Car Park Barriers (Boom Gates)

High-frequency, high-traffic entry points. Multi-million cycle hydraulics with <3s open time. Best for MCST car parks and corporate basements.

Typical Cost
$4,000 – $12,000 per lane

Auto Gates (Swing/Slide)

Lower-frequency residential entry where aesthetics and full perimeter closure matter. Swing, slide, or cantilever configurations available.

Typical Cost
$3,000 – $15,000 per gate
