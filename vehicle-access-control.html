The Philosophy

Vehicle Access: Authorisation by Purpose

Vehicle access control isn't about installing boom gates — it's about answering three fundamental questions:

Who is the driver?

Separating authorised users (tenants, employees, fleet) from temporary visitors requiring one-time approval.

What credential does the vehicle carry?

Matching technology: license plates (ANPR), IU tags, long-range RFID, or guard-verified intercom entry.

How do we prove and log it?

Creating audit trails showing vehicle plate, timestamp, entry/exit records, and who authorised access.

Two Distinct User Groups: How We Design

Our design approach architects separate authorisation paths based on the vehicle's relationship with the property.

🚗

Authorised Vehicles

Daily Access with Ongoing Credentials

Who They Are

  • Residents & homeowners (registered vehicles)
  • Tenants (commercial, industrial lease holders)
  • Employees (corporate fleet, staff parking)
  • Company vehicles (logistics, service fleet)

Access Strategies

  • 🚘 ANPR: License plate recognition, touchless and fast.
  • 📡 IU Tags (Singapore): Built-in ERP readers for season pass.
  • 📻 UHF Long-Range: 5-10m read distance, no stopping.
  • 🏷️ RFID Stickers: Low-cost windshield-mount credentials.
🚕

Temporary Vehicles

Supervised or Time-Limited Entry

The Tiers

  • Tier 1: Casual visitors (friends, family)
  • Tier 2: Contractors (regular maintenance, cleaners)
  • Tier 3: Delivery vehicles (couriers, Grab Food, Shopee)
  • Tier 4: Ride-hailing (Grab, taxis, pick-up/drop-off)

Access Strategies

  • 📞 Video Intercom: Visual verification before gate release.
  • 📋 Pre-Registration: Host generates temp plate approval.
  • 🏷️ Temp RFID: Paper/cardboard tags that expire.
  • 👮 Guard Override: Manual approval via dashboard.

"This isn't about installing boom gates everywhere. It's about architecting credential strategies — matching the right detection technology to each vehicle type's entry pattern and security requirements."

How It Works

Why Vehicle Credential Systems Matter: Three Scenarios

Manual gate control and audio-only intercoms create significant security gaps. Credential-based vehicle authorisation eliminates them through automated identification and audit logging.

Vulnerability: High Risk

Shared Remotes

  • Office gives one remote to all staff
  • Ex-employees still have copies
  • Remote lent to unauthorised drivers
  • No log of who entered when

Security Gap: Remote sharing leads to complete loss of accountability. Anyone with a cloned remote can enter 24/7.

Vulnerability: Blind Entry

Audio-Only Intercom

  • Driver presses button: "Delivery for Unit 12"
  • Guard hears voice, opens barrier
  • No visual of vehicle or driver
  • No plate number logged

Security Gap: Audio verification is easily faked. Unauthorised vehicles claim to be delivery and enter undetected.

Solution: Full Accountability

ANPR + Video Intercom

  • ANPR reads plate automatically (authorised = auto-open)
  • Unknown plates trigger video intercom alert
  • Guard sees vehicle + driver via camera
  • System logs plate, timestamp, guard approval

Result: Complete vehicle audit trail. Authorised vehicles enter touchlessly. Visitors verified visually before entry.

Credential Technologies

How Singapore Vehicles Are Identified

We deploy six primary credential technologies, each suited to different vehicle types, traffic volumes, and security requirements.

ANPR (License Plate Recognition)

How It Works

AI-powered cameras read Singapore plates (SBA1234A format) at 5-50 km/h. Matches against whitelist/blacklist database.

Best For

  • • Condominiums (resident vehicles)
  • • Commercial car parks (tenant vehicles)
  • • Industrial yards (fleet vehicles)

Limitations

Requires clean, well-lit plate visibility. Rain/dirt can reduce accuracy (solved with IR illuminators).

Typical Cost
$3,000 – $8,000 per lane

IU Tag Readers (Singapore ERP System)

How It Works

Reads In-Vehicle Unit (IU) tags used for ERP tolls. Same tech as LTA gantries. Unique ID per vehicle.

Best For

  • • Season parking (HDB, commercial)
  • • Properties near ERP zones
  • • Integration with existing IU infrastructure

Singapore-Specific Advantage

Every car already has IU installed (mandatory for ERP). No need to issue new credentials.

Typical Cost
$4,000 – $10,000 per lane

UHF Long-Range RFID

How It Works

Windshield-mounted RFID tags read at 5-10 meters. Vehicle doesn't slow down. Fast, reliable, affordable.

Best For

  • • High-traffic commercial car parks
  • • Industrial sites (employee vehicles)
  • • Gated estates (resident vehicles)

Advantages

Works in all weather. No camera maintenance. Low cost per tag ($5-$15). Can combine with ANPR for dual-verification.

Typical Cost
$2,500 – $6,000 per lane

RFID Stickers (Passive Tags)

How It Works

Passive stickers (no battery) read at 1-3 meters. Driver taps or drives close to reader. Very low cost.

Best For

  • • Small properties (landed homes, shophouses)
  • • Budget-conscious deployments
  • • Temporary contractor vehicles

Limitations

Short read range requires slowing down. Stickers can peel off over time.

Typical Cost
$1,500 – $3,500 per gate

Video Intercom (Guard-Verified Entry)

How It Works

Driver presses button. Guard sees vehicle via camera, verifies purpose, manually logs plate, opens barrier.

Best For

  • • Visitor lanes (separate from resident lanes)
  • • Delivery vehicle verification
  • • Properties with 24/7 guard presence

When To Use

Backup for unknown vehicles when ANPR doesn't recognise plate. Essential for mixed-use properties.

Typical Cost
$2,000 – $5,000 per station

Pre-Registration (App/Web Portal)

How It Works

Residents/tenants pre-register visitor plates via app. System whitelists plate for specific date/time. Auto-entry.

Best For

  • • Condominiums (resident guests)
  • • Commercial buildings (meeting visitors)
  • • Advance notification scenarios

User Experience

Resident sends WhatsApp: "Guest ABC1234X visiting tomorrow 2PM." System adds to whitelist. Guest enters touchlessly.

Typical Cost
Included in VESTA™ platform

Physical Barriers

Car Park Barriers vs Auto Gates

The credential technology identifies the vehicle. The barrier mechanism controls physical access. We deploy different barrier types based on duty cycle, space constraints, and aesthetic requirements.

Car Park Barriers (Boom Gates)

Design Purpose

High-frequency, high-traffic entry points where vehicles queue and credentials are verified per vehicle.

Typical Use Cases

  • Condominium car park entry/exit
  • Commercial building basement access
  • Industrial yard entry (loading bays)
  • HDB season parking gantries

Mechanism Options

  • Electro-mechanical (150,000+ cycles, <3s open)
  • Hydraulic (heavy-duty, 1,000,000+ cycles)
  • Folding arm (space-constrained areas)

Safety Features

  • Infrared sensors (prevent arm-drop)
  • Manual release (fire/power failure)
  • Breakaway arm (collision protection)
Typical Cost
$4,000 – $12,000 per lane

Auto Gates (Swing/Slide Gates)

Design Purpose

Lower-frequency, residential entry where aesthetics and full perimeter closure matter more than speed.

Typical Use Cases

  • Landed property driveways
  • Semi-detached/terrace homes
  • Private estate gated entrances
  • Industrial facility perimeter gates

Mechanism Options

  • Swing gates (hinged, traditional aesthetic)
  • Sliding gates (space-efficient)
  • Cantilever gates (no ground track)

Safety Features

  • Photocells (prevent closing on people)
  • Battery backup (power cuts)
  • Obstacle detection (auto-reverse)
Typical Cost
$3,000 – $15,000 per gate

Choosing Between Barriers and Gates

  • High Traffic (>100 vehicles/day) → Barriers
  • Low Traffic (<50 vehicles/day) → Gates
  • Aesthetic Priority (residential) → Gates
  • Speed Priority (commercial) → Barriers
  • Full Perimeter Closure → Gates
  • Single Lane Control → Barriers

GantryGo™ Visitor Intercom System

GantryGo™ is Securevision's purpose-built gantry intercom platform — designed specifically for the visitor management challenges unique to Singapore condominiums and gated properties.

When a visitor arrives, they call the resident via the gantry intercom. The resident sees a live video feed and can open the barrier directly from their smartphone — from anywhere. Every visitor call is logged with a photo and timestamp.

  • No security guard intervention required for resident-authorised visitors
  • Pre-booking allows automatic barrier release for expected visitors
