1. Core Philosophy
The Securevision Design Framework is a 4-pillar architectural standard designed to build "Systems" authority through technical education and engineering logic. It transforms a visitor into an informed stakeholder by teaching them the "How" and "Why" behind security technology, moving from "products" to "engineered ecosystems."

2. The 4 Pillars of the Securevision Design Framework

I. Surveillance & Detection ("We need to see and detect whatever we are worried about.")
The Core Logic: Proactive awareness is the foundation of any defense. Security begins with understanding your threats and detecting them before they cross your perimeter.
Technical Anatomy: Moving from simple "recording" to intelligent "active surveillance."
Components: CCTV & Video Analytics (AI-driven detection) and Perimeter Security (Burglar Alarms, heat sensors, vibration detection).

II. Controlling People Access ("Prevent damage and sabotage by restricting access.")
The Core Logic: You must have total control over your physical perimeter. A secure site manages the flow of authorized personnel and strictly blocks unauthorized entry.
Technical Anatomy: Transitioning from mechanical keys to secure, non-replicable digital identity.
Components: Access Control & Biometrics (Fingerprint, Facial, RFID) and Video Intercoms (Visitor management, delivery verification).

III. Controlling Vehicle Access ("Managing assets that cross your boundary.")
The Core Logic: A boundary isn't just for foot traffic. Managing the speed, flow, and authorization of vehicles is a separate and critical security layer, often requiring high-torque mechanics and unique detection technology.
Technical Anatomy: Designing for safety and duty cycle (frequency of use).
Components: Auto Gates & Barrier Systems (Hydraulic and electro-mechanical) and LPR (License Plate Recognition) (Automated vehicle identification).

IV. Platform Management ("A platform that can integrate human actions.")
The Core Logic: Fragmented systems create vulnerability. The platform is the central nervous system, breaking down silos, automating responses, and creating the definitive audit trail required for compliance.
Technical Anatomy: Building "If-This-Then-That" logic between physical devices (S, P, V) and human actions.
Components: Integration Software (VESTA, central dashboard) and Response & Documentation (Central Monitoring signals, push alerts, detailed log reports).

3. Standardized Page Structure
All educational system pages must follow this 5-part modular sequence to maintain the Architect standard:

PART 1. The Conceptual Logic (The "Why")
Define the high-level security objective using the 4-pillar framework. Move beyond "product" to "purpose."

PART 2. The Anatomy of the Tech (The "How")
Deconstruct the hardware. Explain the physics of sensors (e.g., PIR heat detection) or the mechanics of motors. Use technical diagrams rather than lifestyle photos.

PART 3. The Securevision Design Standard (The "Expertise")
Singapore-specific engineering experience. SCDF fire safety (Fail-Safe logic), MAS TRM compliance, or IP67-rated tropicalization.

PART 4. System Integration & Response (The "Unification")
Demonstrate how the individual pillar talks to the others. Explain cross-system triggers (e.g., how an Intercom "Entry" event triggers a CCTV "Surveillance" recording).

PART 5. The Architect’s Checklist (The "Action")
Provide a consultative self-audit. 5-6 technical questions that prompt the user to request a professional site assessment.
