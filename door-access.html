3. What is a Door Access Control System?

Electronic access control replaces traditional mechanical keys with digital authentication, centralized management, and electric locking mechanisms.

Unlike mechanical locks, digital systems offer:

  • Full Audit Trails: Know exactly who entered which room and when.
  • Instant Revocation: Blacklist lost cards or ex-employees in seconds.
  • Time-Based Access: Restrict entry to specific hours (e.g., office hours only).
  • Zero Key Duplication: Prevent unauthorized copies of physical keys.

4. How Door Access Systems Work

The Five Core Components

A professional installation involves five critical layers working in sequence:

1. Credentials: Cards, fingers, or faces.
2. Readers: Scanners mounted outside.
3. Controllers: The hidden 'Brain'.
4. Electric Locks: EM locks or strikes.
5. Software: The management hub.

6. Single Door Access Control

Typically used for side gates or individual secure rooms. These use integrated readers with built-in controllers. All programming is done via keypad or a direct mobile connection.

Ideal for: Condo side gates, private office rooms, and server closets.

7. Multi-Door Access Control

Professional multi-door systems use dedicated controller boards (housed in a secure enclosure) to manage multiple reader-equipped doors. This logic separation is much more secure, as tampering with the outer reader won't expose the lock wiring.

12. Lock Selection & Hardware

Lock choice is dictated by the door material and traffic volume:

  • EM Locks (600kg/1200kg): Best for glass doors.
  • Electric Strikes: Fits into wood/metal frames.
  • Electric Drop Bolts: Strong motorized locking for double doors.

28. Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use my existing cards?

Most modern readers are multi-tech and can read legacy 125kHz and secure 13.56MHz cards.

What happens if power fails?

System runs for 4 hours on UPS. Fail-safe locks unlock automatically for safety.
