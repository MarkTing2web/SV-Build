The Context

The Challenge: Modernizing Security for a High-Traffic Youth Hub

In July 2022, *SCAPE announced a complete rebrand and facility overhaul to better serve Singapore's youth community. As a prominent youth hub located in the heart of Orchard Road's Somerset Belt, the facility required a complete security system modernization to support its reopening in 2024.

The project presented several unique challenges:

Complex Multi-Level Facility Management

The six-level facility — spanning basement parking through rooftop access — serves multiple functions simultaneously. Public youth spaces coexist with bookable private rooms, restricted operational areas, and high-traffic event venues. Each level required different security approaches while maintaining seamless integration. The existing fragmented system couldn't provide the unified visibility and control that modern facility management demands.

High Foot Traffic & Safety Intelligence

As a youth-focused public facility in prime Orchard Road, *SCAPE experiences significant daily foot traffic with diverse visitor profiles. Management needed real-time crowd monitoring and behavior analytics to ensure safety without creating an intrusive security presence. Traditional motion-based surveillance generated too many false alarms, overwhelming security staff and delaying genuine incident response. The facility required intelligent video analytics that could distinguish between normal activity and actual security events.

Operational Efficiency Requirements

The existing system relied heavily on manual processes — physical logbooks for visitor registration, standalone access cards that could be lost or shared, and fragmented monitoring across multiple platforms. Security staff spent more time on administrative tasks than actual security monitoring. *SCAPE needed comprehensive automation and system unification to reduce guard workload while improving operational responsiveness.

Technology Integration Complexity

The facility uses Salesforce for room bookings and event management. The new security system needed to integrate seamlessly — automatically generating access credentials for confirmed bookings, managing visitor pre-registration, and handling on-site contractor access — all without requiring security staff intervention for routine operations. The visitor management system needed to support both planned access (pre-booked visitors receiving QR codes) and unplanned access (contractors registering on-site), while maintaining complete audit trails for compliance.

The Approach

Our Solution: Unified AI-Powered Security Architecture

Securevision designed and deployed a comprehensive integrated security system built on three core pillars: AI-powered visual intelligence, automated access management, and unified operational control.

Intelligent Visual Surveillance

We deployed 209 Milesight AI cameras across all six levels, strategically positioned through pre-installation simulation modeling to eliminate blind spots while maintaining unobtrusive coverage. The system uses four camera types optimized for specific environments:

167 dome cameras provide general indoor coverage with built-in people counting and behavior detection. These 5-megapixel units feature edge AI processing, analyzing video locally to detect specific events — region entrance and exit, line crossing, loitering, object removal — and alerting security staff only when genuine incidents occur, eliminating the false alarm fatigue common in motion-only systems.

8 panoramic 180-degree cameras secure the perimeter, providing seamless coverage that would traditionally require multiple conventional cameras. These 8-megapixel units combine thermal and visible-light sensors with AI-powered intrusion detection, distinguishing between humans, vehicles, and environmental motion like foliage or weather patterns.

33 vandal-proof cameras with IK10 impact resistance protect high-risk areas requiring enhanced physical security. 2 specialized bullet cameras handle outdoor monitoring with enhanced low-light performance using Sony STARVIS technology, capturing color images at 0.008 lux.

All cameras feature edge AI processing, analyzing video locally rather than sending everything to central servers. This distributed intelligence approach reduces network bandwidth requirements, enables faster alert response, and ensures the system continues functioning even if network connectivity is temporarily disrupted.

Automated Access Control & Visitor Management

Rather than relying on physical access cards or manual logbooks, we implemented 37 ZKTeco SpeedFace V4L Pro-QR readers throughout the facility. Staff and regular visitors use face recognition for seamless access — no cards to lose, forget, or share. Event attendees and room renters receive dynamic QR codes automatically generated when they complete their Salesforce booking, with no security desk interaction required.

The integrated visitor management system handles both planned and unplanned access scenarios. For planned visits, the pre-booking portal allows visitors to restricted areas to submit requests online, receive approval from *SCAPE management, and automatically receive time-limited QR codes for specific zones and durations. For unplanned visits, contractors and service providers register at the Facilities Control Center, where security staff generate instant access credentials for designated areas.

The Salesforce integration creates a seamless booking-to-access workflow. When a customer confirms a room booking and payment is received, Salesforce sends booking data to the ZKBio access control platform. The system cross-references the booked room with date, time, and duration parameters, then generates a unique QR code valid only for that specific access point and time window. Upon booking cancellation, the system immediately revokes associated QR codes in real-time, preventing unauthorized access from cancelled reservations.

Centralized Intelligence Platform

Everything converges in the Facilities Control Center, where two Milesight VE1208 Enterprise VMS servers aggregate all camera feeds, access events, and system health data into a unified operational dashboard. The Windows-based platform provides capabilities that conventional network video recorders cannot deliver.

Cloud backup integration satisfies *SCAPE's specific regulatory compliance requirements — footage automatically replicates to cloud storage, ensuring disaster recovery capability and audit trail preservation beyond local storage limits. Advanced search and forensics use AI-powered video synopsis technology, where hours of footage condense to minutes by superimposing moving objects onto a stationary background with timestamps. Incidents that would traditionally require hours of manual video scrubbing can now be located in seconds.

Real-time dashboards display facility occupancy levels, access control violations, and system health status across all levels from a single interface. Security staff monitor 200+ cameras simultaneously, receive intelligent alerts filtered by AI to prioritize genuine incidents over routine motion, and can retrieve forensic footage almost instantly using smart search rather than linear playback.

The API integration with Salesforce completes the automated credential lifecycle — booking confirmation triggers access creation, time windows are enforced automatically, and booking cancellation immediately revokes credentials, all without manual security staff intervention for routine operations.
