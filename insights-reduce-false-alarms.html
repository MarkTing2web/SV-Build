How to Reduce False Alarms

Around 80% of burglar alarm activations in Singapore are false alarms. A system that "cries wolf" is a system that eventually gets ignored or switched off entirely. Reducing false alarms is a systematic process of matching technology to environment.

1. User error — the #1 cause

The vast majority of false alarms occur during entry or exit. Someone enters the property and forgets to disarm, or fails to notice a window was left open before arming. System design can mitigate this:

  • Forced Arming Prevention: Configure the system so it cannot be armed if a zone is "open" (like a balcony door). The keypad should beep specifically to tell you which door is the problem.
  • Automated Schedules: For commercial offices, set an "Auto-Disarm" ten minutes before the first staff member usually arrives. This prevents them from walking into an armed building while distracted.

2. Thermal turbulence and sunlight

Standard PIR (Passive Infrared) motion sensors look for heat movement. In Singapore, a common cause of false alarms is the afternoon sun heating up a floor or furniture through a window. As the heat rises, it creates "thermal turbulence" that the sensor interprets as a person.

The solution is not to turn down the sensitivity (which makes the system less secure) but to use Dual-Technology sensors. These sensors require both infrared heat AND microwave movement to trigger an alarm simultaneously. This virtually eliminates sun-related false activations.

3. Insects and dusty environments

Small insects crawling inside a PIR sensor can look like a giant intruder to the sensor's optics. Similarly, dust accumulation on the lens can create diffraction patterns that trigger the sensor. Professional installers use "Insect-Proof" or "Sealed Optics" sensors for environments like warehouses or ground-floor units where pests are common.

ENGINEERING NOTE

If you have pets, you MUST use pet-tolerant sensors. A 20kg dog moving at floor level will trigger a standard sensor 100% of the time. Pet-tolerant sensors are designed to ignore mass below a certain height or weight, keeping the house secure while the dog is inside.

4. Using Video Verification

The most effective way to manage false alarms is video verification. Modern systems like AJAX or Hikvision link the alarm trigger to a camera. When the alarm goes off, the system pushes a 10-second video clip to your phone. You can immediately see if it's a burglar or just a curtain blowing in the wind, allowing you to dismiss the alarm without a site visit.

A false alarm is a bug in your security system. If your alarm triggers more than once a year without a valid reason, the system needs to be re-engineered. Repositioning a single sensor or switching one PIR to Dual-Tech is usually all it takes to restore your confidence in the system.
